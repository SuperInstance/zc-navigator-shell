# Cycle 100 — Navigator
**Task:** Scan SuperInstance repos for READMEs that need improvement. List the 10 worst ones.

---

### Step 1 — Map the negative space (P0)
**Rocks:**
- Don't assume all repos are under `/home` — some may be in `/mnt`, `/opt`, or other mounts.
- Don't rely on file extensions alone; READMEs may be `.md`, `.txt`, or have no extension.
- Don't evaluate repos I cannot read due to permissions.
- Don't include repos that are intentionally minimal (e.g., placeholder, archived).
- Don't rank based solely on length; a short, clear README may be sufficient.
- Don't process binary files or non-text files as READMEs.
- Don't assume English; but for fleet consistency, English is the standard.

### Step 2 — Identify safe channels (P1)
**Safe channels:**
- Scan known fleet directories from `fleet-knowledge/` and common paths (`/home`, `/mnt`, `/opt`).
- Use `find` with case-insensitive name patterns (`README*`, `readme*`).
- Filter to text files using `file` command or extension.
- Apply a scoring heuristic: presence of key sections (Description, Installation, Usage), length, clarity, and update date.
- Compare against fleet README standard inferred from `fleet-knowledge/docs/`.
- Focus on active, non-archived repos (no `.archive`, `deprecated` in path).

### Step 3 — Optimize within channel (P2)
**Execution:**
1. **Locate repos:** Search for READMEs in likely SuperInstance directories.
2. **Extract metadata:** For each README, capture: repo path, file size, last modified date, and a quick content sample.
3. **Score heuristically:** Assign penalty points for:
   - Missing entirely (auto-worst).
   - Very small (< 200 bytes).
   - No clear structure (no headings, bullet points).
   - Outdated (e.g., "TODO", "coming soon", old dates).
   - Lack of installation/usage instructions.
   - Generic placeholder text.
4. **Rank:** Sort by penalty score descending, take top 10.
5. **Validate:** Ensure they are indeed SuperInstance/fleet repos.
