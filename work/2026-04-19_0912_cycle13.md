### Step 3 — Optimize within channel (P2)
**Execution path:**
1. Locate READMEs in fleet directories.
2. For each, compute a quick quality score based on:
   - **Length:** < 200 chars = poor.
   - **Structure:** Missing standard headers (## Installation, ## Usage) = poor.
   - **Clarity:** Contains placeholder text (TBD, TODO, "coming soon") = poor.
   - **Examples:** No code examples or configuration snippets = poor.
3. Rank the worst 10 by composite score.
4. Output list with repo path and specific deficiencies.

## Recommendations for Scribe (Phase 2 Task)

These 10 READMEs are prime candidates for the Scribe's "Rewrite 3 fleet READMEs to standard format" task. The standard format should include:

1. **Project Title & One-Liner**
2. **Status** (Active/Experimental/Deprecated)
3. **Purpose** (How it fits into fleet architecture)
4. **Installation** (Step-by-step, with prerequisites)
5. **Usage** (Basic example with code)
6. **API/Configuration** (Key options)
7. **Integration** (How it connects to other fleet components)
8. **Contributing** (Link to fleet contribution guidelines)

Prioritize `flux-runtime`, `constraint-theory-core`, and `holodeck-rust` as they are core technologies with near-empty READMEs.
